Introduction

BALTEK is a turn-based board game, inspired from football. Here are its principles:

  • Game easily made by hand,
  • defined by a few rules,
  • without randomizing device.
  • The game allows several actions per turn.
  • The ball moves faster than the footballers.
  • The coordination of footballers is essential.

The name of the game is built using sounds of the words ball (BAL) and technical (TEK).

Material

Here is the material, shown in the image above:

  • 1 white ball, with the label @.
  • A team of 6 blue footballers.
  • A team of 6 red footballers.
  • Each team consists of:
    • 1 centre forward of force 3.
    • 2 wingers of force 2.
    • 3 fullbacks of force 1.
  • The field is built using 52 green squares.
  • The square a3, with the label #, is the goal defended by the blue team.
  • The square l3, with the label #, is the goal defended by the red team.

The coordinates and the labels, @ and #, are used for notation of moves.

Purpose

  • BALTEK is played by two players.
  • Each player leads a team of 6 footballers.
  • In turn, each player moves the ball and his/her footballers, trying to send the ball into the opponent's goal.
  • The winner is the player who first scored 2 goals.

How to start

  • The players decide the leader of each team.
  • The blue team always starts the match.
  • At the beginning of the game, as well as after each scored goal, the footballers move at their kickoff positions.
  • The kickoff positions are fixed.
  • The kickoff positions of the two teams are symmetrical, except those of the centre forwards.
  • The team that has just scored a goal lets the other team kicks off.

How to play

Actions, options, compensations, credits and bonuses

  • In turn, each player chooses actions and options, which, depending on the context, require compensations.
  • These choices cost credits or bonuses.
  • Each player receives:
    • 3 credits, at the beginning of each turn;
    • 1 bonus, at each kickoff.
  • In turn, each player can spend 0, 1, 2 or 3 credits, and 0 or 1 bonus.
  • The actions, options and compensations are as follows:
    • Run: move a footballer; cost: 1 credit.
    • Sprint: option to increase the move of a footballer; cost: 1 bonus.
    • Shoot: move the ball; cost: 1 credit.
    • Dribble: compensation of force to own the ball; cost: 1 credit per unit of force.
    • Effect: compensation of force to move the ball; cost: 1 credit per unit of force.

Action run

  • The action run costs 1 credit and allows to move a footballer by 1 square.
  • Each square of the zone 3 x 3, centered on the moved footballer, is a possible destination, unless:
    • it is outside the field;
    • it is a goal square;
    • it already hosts a footballer of the same team.
  • Each footballer can only run once per turn.
  • The action run does not move the ball.

Example: the possible destinations for the blue 3 footballer have yellow borders.

Option sprint

  • The option sprint costs 1 bonus and extends to 2 squares the move of a footballer.
  • The option sprint must always be combined with the action run.
  • Each square of the 5 x 5 zone, centered on the moved footballer, is a possible destination, unless:
    • it is outside the field;
    • it is a goal square;
    • it already hosts a footballer of the same team.
  • The player can only spend his/her bonus once between two kickoffs.

Example: the possible destinations for the blue 3 footballer have yellow borders.

Action shoot

  • The actions transmit the ball to a footballer, send the ball to an empty square and shoot at the goal obey the same rules.
  • The action shoot costs 1 credit. If the general conditions and the force conditions are satisfied, the action allows to move the ball up to 2 squares along horizontal, vertical or diagonal directions. However, the trajectory of the ball is never curved.
  • Here are the general conditions to be met:
    • The player has a shooter in the starting square of the ball.
    • In this turn, the shooter has not already shot.
    • The arriving square of the ball is in the field.
    • The arriving square of the ball is not the goal defended by the player.
    • The arriving square of the ball may be empty or not.
  • Here are the force conditions to be met:
    • If the shooter shares his/ber square with an opponent, then the shooter must:
      • either equal or surpass the force of his/her opponent,
      • or compensate for his/ber inferiority by a dribble.
    • If the shooter moves the ball over an opponent, then the shooter must:
      • either equal or surpass the force of his/her opponent,
      • or compensate for his/her inferiority by an effect.

Example: the possible destinations for the ball by the blue 3 shooter have yellow borders; none of these destinations requires dribble or effect.

Compensation by dribble

  • The action shoot may be require this compensation .
  • The dribble compensates for the inferiority of the shooter with respect to the opponent sharing his/her square.
  • The dribble costs 1 credit per unit of compensated force.

Examples: to own the ball, the blue 2 shooter must engage a dribble costing 1 credit; and the blue 1 shooter must engage a dribble costing 2 credits.

Compensation by effect

  • The action shoot may be require this compensation .
  • The effect compensates for the inferiority of the shooter with respect to the opponent over which the ball passes.
  • The effect costs 1 credit per unit of compensated force.
  • The compensation by effect is not necessary to pass over a footballer of his/her team.

Examples: to transmit the ball to the blue 2 footballer, the blue 1 shooter must perform an effect costing 1 credit in the left context and an effect costing 2 credits in the right context.

Notation of moves

If useful, the moves of the game are noted as in the following example.

@d3  f3g3 d1e1  / h3g3 g5f5 i3h3
e1e2 d5e4 f5g4  / f5e4 g1f1 i1h2
@e4  @g4+       / g3g4 i5i4 @i4
...
@# /

Optionally, annotations are added as in the following example.

#! BALTEK 1.1
#@ bernard@bois.fr robert@riviere.fr
#: 2014-12-24 11:09:30
@d3  f3g3 d1e1  / h3g3 g5f5 i3h3
e1e2 d5e4 f5g4  / f5e4 g1f1 i1h2
@e4  @g4+       / g3g4 i5i4 @i4
...
@# /
#: 2014-12-24 11:30:00
# The red bonus has been well played.

The notation is structured by rows. Here are its conventions:

  • The space character , optionally repeated, separates the words.
  • A line beginning with # is a comment or pseudo-comment, and is always optional.
    • # , sharp followed by space, announces a free comment.
    • #! BALTEK 1.1 announces the version 1.1 of the BALTEK notation format; usable only once, in the first row.
    • #@ announces the identifiers chosen by the two players, in order blue then red; usable only once.
    • #: announces, using an ISO format, the date, then optionally the time; usable several times.
  • A non-empty line, which is not a comment, describes a blue turn, followed by a red turn.
  • / ends the blue turn. The end of the line ends the red turn .
  • Each turn is composed of 0, 1, 2 ou 3 actions.
    • The action run is noted using one word beginning with the starting square and ending with the arriving square. Example: g3f3
    • For noting the sprint, * is appended to the action word. Example: g3e2*
    • The action shot is noted using one word beginning with @ and ending with the arriving square. Example: @c3
    • The action shot at goal is abbreviated as follows: @#
    • For a dribble costing 1 credit, + is appended to the action word. Example: @c3+
    • For a dribble costing 2 credits, ++ is appended to the action word. Example: @c3++
    • For an effect costing 1 credit, ! is appended to the action word. Example: @c3!
    • For an effect costing 2 credits, !! is appended to the action word. Example: @c3!!
    • For a combination dribble and effect, each one costing 1 credit, +! is appended to the action word. Example: @c3+!

Indexes of versions

  • The versions of the rules are identified by two integer indices i.j.
  • The first version is 1.0.
  • When the logic of the rules changes, the first index i is incremented by 1, and the second index j is reset to 0.
  • The second index j is incremented by 1 when the texts or the illustrations or the notation of moves or the structure of the document evolve, or a translation is added, but without changing the logic of the rules.
